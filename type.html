Facts About Flowers

Sunflowers

Sunflowers first originated around 1000 BC in the Americas. They were first grown as a food source, using the seeds for flour to make bread. The seeds were also eaten as a snack. They also used sunflower oil for their skin and hair. There are different colored sunflowers including yellow, red, orange, white, pink, and purple. Sunflowers follow the sun as it moves from east to west during the day.

Roses

Roses have originated in Cental Asia and spread throughout the northern hemisphere. Ecuador is one of the world's largest suppliers of roses, with 54% of the land filled with roses. There is natural light all year round that allows the rose to thrive with longer growing cycles. Roses are one of the oldest flowers. There are red, white, yellow, pink, orange, blue, lavendar, and black roses.

Daisies

Daisies were among the flowers that grew in ancient Egyptian temple gardens from around 2,2000 B.C. Daisies are flowering plants with flat, disc-shaped blooms. They are part of the Asteraceae family along with chrysanthemums, zinnias, and sunflowers. Daisy leaves are consumable and high in vitamin C. Each daisy is actually two flowers in one. The outside petals are one flower and the central disc is another.

Lillies

Lillies originated in the northern hemisphere, likely in the Himalayas. They are among the world's oldest cultivated plants. Lillies are perennial plants, meaning they can survive and grow without much human intervention. They are non-toxic to humans but are poisonous to cats. Only white and tiger lillies are known by their beautiful smell. Other species of lily are odorloss. Some species of lillies can be used as food. Bulbs of lily contain a lot of starch and are used as root vegetables.

Tulips

Tulips are native to Central Asia, where they thrive in the harsh winters and hot summers. Tulips can keep growing even after being cut from their stem. Some tulip petals are edible but not all tulip varities are safe for consumption. There are more than 3,000 varities of tulips worldwide and they come in every color except blue.
